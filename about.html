FOUNDER

SUJA MANOJ

Suja Raghuram Manoj is an Indian entrepreneur and the founder of Vastri Infiniti, a company that specializes in animation and VFX services. She is known for her innovative approach in bringing world-class animation and visual effects to the Indian market, helping to elevate the entertainment and media industry in the country.

Suja Raghuram Manoj’s Background

Suja Raghuram Manoj is an Indian actress and director, renowned for her contributions to both Indian cinema and international projects. Born to the legendary dance choreographer K.S. Raghuram and Girija Raghuram, Suja was immersed in the world of performing arts from a young age. Her sister, Gayathri Raghuram, is also a prominent figure in the industry.
Early Life and Background
Suja hails from a family deeply rooted in the arts. Her father, K.S. Raghuram, was a celebrated dance choreographer who worked across various Indian film industries. This familial environment provided Suja with a rich cultural upbringing, influencing her eventual foray into acting and direction.
Acting Career
In the early 2000s, Suja ventured into acting, appearing in films such as "Millennium Stars" and "Krishnadasi." Her performances garnered attention, establishing her as a talented actress in the industry.
Directorial Debut
Transitioning from acting, Suja made her directorial debut with the Hollywood film "Take It Easy." Shot in the USA, this English-language film is a light-hearted narrative focusing on the theme of friendship among three youngsters. Notably, the film features her children, Sana and Trishul Manoj, in lead roles. The music for "Take It Easy" includes a special song composed by Sam CS, sung by Benny Dayal and Sana Manoj. The film was released on the Mass TV app on September 10, 2021.
Personal Life
Suja is married to Manoj Venugopal, who produced "Take It Easy." The couple has three children, Trishul, Sana and Vara, all three of whom have been involved in their mother's directorial projects.
Dancing Career: A Global Presence Suja Raghuram is an exceptional dancer who has performed in over 300 stage shows across the world under the mentorship of some of India's most celebrated choreographers, including: Raghuram Master (her father, a legendary choreographer) Kala Master Brindha Master Her performances have taken her to various countries, where she has showcased Indian dance forms on global platforms, earning her recognition and admiration. Her dedication to dance has not only brought her fame but has also upheld the legacy of her father’s contribution to the Indian film industry.

The Story Behind Vastri Infiniti

Vastri Infiniti was founded by Suja with a clear vision: to provide cutting-edge animation and VFX services to the Indian entertainment industry and beyond. From the outset, the company’s goal was not just to produce stunning visuals, but to transform storytelling through animation, VFX, and visual artistry. The road to success was not without challenges. Building a world-class animation and VFX studio meant not only gathering a talented team but also convincing an industry often skeptical about India's capability in high-end digital content. At that time, many still viewed Indian animation as underdeveloped, primarily suited for children’s content. Suja sought to change this perception and create an industry standard where animation and VFX were integral to both blockbuster cinema and high-quality television programming. The breakthrough for Vastri Infiniti came when the company started collaborating with major film production houses, delivering VFX for high-profile Bollywood films and animation for a variety of TV shows. Their work was not only visually stunning but also emotionally resonant, proving that animation and VFX were not merely special effects but an essential part of compelling storytelling.

The Role of Animation and VFX in Indian Cinema

Suja was a pioneer in pushing the idea that animation and VFX could go beyond traditional boundaries in Indian cinema. Prior to companies like Vastri Infiniti entering the scene, animation in India was mainly used for children's cartoons or limited, budget-friendly projects. Suja, however, was determined to prove that animation and VFX could be applied across a wide range of genres and films—ranging from children’s entertainment to high-budget, visually intricate projects for mature audiences. Her company helped to redefine the Indian animation and VFX landscape by not just focusing on technological advancement, but also on the emotional and narrative depth that visuals could bring to a project. Suja knew that in order to compete with international studios, Vastri Infiniti needed to excel in both artistry and technical execution.

Overcoming Challenges

Building a successful animation and VFX company in India was not without its hurdles. Suja faced competition from both local and international firms, as well as financial and resource-related constraints. Keeping up with the constantly evolving technological landscape was also a challenge—animation and VFX technology changes rapidly, and staying ahead of the curve requires significant investment in both hardware and software. Despite these challenges, Suja’s commitment to excellence, quality, and innovation helped Vastri Infiniti become a respected name in the animation and VFX industry. Her ability to blend creative talent with cutting-edge technology set the company apart, and it didn’t take long for Vastri Infiniti to attract attention from top-tier production houses both in India and abroad.

A Legacy of Innovation and Growth

Today, Vastri Infiniti stands as a testament to Suja Raghuram Manoj’s vision and determination. The company has worked on a diverse range of projects, from animated series and films to complex visual effects for major Bollywood and international projects. Suja’s focus on creativity, collaboration, and high standards has not only helped elevate the quality of Indian animation and VFX, but also fostered a greater appreciation for visual storytelling. Under her leadership, Vastri Infiniti continues to break new ground in the world of animation, VFX, and digital content creation. Suja Raghuram Manoj’s journey is an inspiring one—a story of turning passion into purpose and changing the narrative of Indian animation and VFX. Through Vastri Infiniti, she has contributed significantly to the growth of the entertainment industry, creating new opportunities for artists, technicians, and storytellers in India and abroad.
